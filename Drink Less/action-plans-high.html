Action Plans

Achieving a goal is easier when you’ve got a plan. Planning helps you manage situations when you may be tempted to drink a bit too much. To make the plans work best, be specific and say IF a certain thing happens THEN you’ll do something else.

Here's some examples. Feel free to copy them, amend them or use them as inspiration for your own:

  • IF I’ve had two drinks and I’m offered another, THEN I’ll say “Thank you, I’ll have a juice"
  • IF I reach for the bottle of wine at dinner, THEN I'll choose water instead
  • IF I'm about to walk into the pub, THEN I'll remind myself of my drinking goals
  • IF I feel a bit tipsy, THEN I'll decide to not drink any more

Follow the IF/THEN structure and be as specific as you can when creating these plans. If they’re specific they come to mind more easily, right when you need them.

Why they work
Here comes the science bit. IF/THEN plans (called implementation intentions) seem to work because having a concrete plan for specific situations means the plan can be performed automatically and without conscious effort. As a result you don’t have to rely on willpower, something alcohol is known to weaken.

You might be thinking that implementation intentions are too simple to be effective. However, a review of nearly 100 scientific studies found they “make an important difference to whether or not people achieve their goals”. They help with drinking less too.
